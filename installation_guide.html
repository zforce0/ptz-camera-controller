Step 1: Prepare your Android tablet

  • Ensure your tablet is running Android 6.0 (Marshmallow) or higher
  • Go to Settings â†’ Security and enable "Unknown Sources" to allow installation of apps from sources other than the Play Store
  • Make sure your tablet has sufficient storage space (at least 50MB)

Step 2: Download the APK

  • Scan the QR code above with your tablet's camera
  • If prompted, select "Download" or "Open in Browser"
  • The APK file will begin downloading

Note: If the QR code doesn't work, you can download the APK directly from the URL shown above.

Step 3: Install the app

  • Once the download completes, tap on the notification or find the APK file in your Downloads folder
  • Tap the APK file to begin installation
  • Review the permissions and tap "Install"
  • Wait for the installation to complete, then tap "Open"

Step 4: Connect to the camera server

  • Ensure the PTZ Camera Server is running on your onboard computer (Raspberry Pi or Jetson)
  • Make sure both your tablet and the onboard computer are on the same network
  • Open the PTZ Camera Control app
  • Enter the IP address of your onboard computer and tap "Connect"

Tip: You can find the IP address of your onboard computer by running hostname -I in the terminal.

Step 5: Start controlling your camera

  • Once connected, you'll see the camera controls and video stream
  • Use the pan, tilt, and zoom controls to adjust the camera position
  • Switch between RGB and IR/Thermal camera modes as needed
  • Adjust stream quality settings if you experience lag or performance issues

Troubleshooting Tips

  • Connection issues: Verify that your tablet and onboard computer are on the same network
  • Streaming problems: Try switching to the low-quality stream in settings
  • Control lag: Check your network connection and reduce the control sensitivity in settings
  • App crashes: Ensure your tablet meets the minimum system requirements
